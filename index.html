Why this exists

If you've played enough Catan, you've seen it: someone drops the tiles at random, the 6 and 8 end up neighbors on a wheat-heavy corner, and whoever settles there first basically wins by turn ten. The "random" setup rules in the box don't actually guarantee a fair board.

This generator does. Every board it produces is checked against a set of balance rules, like keeping hot numbers apart and stopping one resource from dominating, so the game is decided by trades, roads, and the occasional brutal robber placement. Not by tile luck.

Prefer a looser board? Every rule is individually configurable, so the generator bends to how your table likes to play.
